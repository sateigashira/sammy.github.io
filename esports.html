Founder/Director Esports at UC Davis

Gaming has always been a part of my life, which is why I didn't hesitate when I came to Davis and saw students interested in Esports but no formal structure to support them. After feeling my way through the esports scene at Davis my first year there, I started an Esports program aimed at providing an opportunity for students to pursue their passion of all things esports and competitive gaming related. This gave birth to Esports at UC Davis which started as a sub division of the general gaming Club on campus. In my first quarter I saw a massive increase in participation from 4-5 teams to over 15 competitive teams in this program.

I wrote rule sets and competitive guidelines as well as a proposal document that I later presented to the UC Davis Vice Chancellor of Student Affairs. Through that I was able to secure a partnership with Student Affairs that allowed the program to grow and become an official Club Sport.

Through my time as Director I learned so much:

  • How to draft and create professional documents including rule books, proposals, and inclusion plans
  • How to plan and host inclusion events such as a Women in Esports Boot Camp
  • How to foster partnerships with Esports programs at other universities across the world
  • How to successfully manage and facilitate over 100 active players
